Straight answers.

Will a wrap damage my paint?

The opposite. Film shields the factory coat from sun, sand and stone chips, and removal by a professional leaves the paint the way we found it. That is why wrapped cars resell well.

How long does a wrap last?

5 to 7 years with proper care in Florida sun. Garage it and hand-wash it and you will be at the top of that range.

Is it really cheaper than repainting?

A quality respray on a premium car costs more than a full wrap, takes longer, and is permanent. Film gives you the color now and the factory paint back whenever you want it.

Can I wrap a leased car?

Yes. The wrap comes off clean before the car goes back. It is the only color change a lease allows.

How do I wash a wrapped car?

Hand wash or touchless, no wax over matte or satin, and skip the high-pressure lance at the edges. We hand you the full care guide at pickup.

How much does a car wrap cost?

Every car is different: body size and shape, the film line you pick, the condition of the paint, trim that has to come off. That is why we quote in person only: bring the car or the paint code and leave with an exact number that does not move later.

What films do you install?

We plot from the full 3M, Avery Dennison, KPMF, Hexis and Oracal catalogs: manufacturer-backed film lines, never no-name rolls. You pick the finish against real swatches at the consult, and every build leaves with the care guide for that exact film.
